About Me

Solving Real Problems
Through Human Empathy

I believe the best digital solutions start not with code, but with deep human understanding. My teaching integrates Stanford d.school methodology with real engineering challenges.

With over 25 years of professional experience in Electronics and academia, I guide students through structured creative processes — from ambiguous problems to working prototypes that genuinely improve lives.

Based at Symbiosis Institute of Technology, Pune, my research focuses on applying Design Thinking within software development cycles, making it practical for the next generation of engineers.

The Method

Five Stages of Design Thinking

A non-linear, iterative framework for solving complex, human-centred problems. Each stage informs and enriches the others.

01
🫀
Empathise
Conduct user interviews, observations, and immersive research to understand real needs beyond stated requirements.
02
🎯
Define
Synthesise findings into a sharp, actionable problem statement — a Point of View that guides creative work.
03
💡
Ideate
Generate a wide spectrum of ideas through structured brainstorming, challenging assumptions and constraints.
04
🔧
Prototype
Build quick, low-fidelity representations to make ideas tangible. Fail fast, learn faster, iterate constantly.
05
✅
Test
Validate solutions with real users, gather qualitative feedback, and return to earlier stages as needed.
